Regularized time ​

The Kerr spacetime admits a unique Killing vector field, ξt, that is timelike at r=∞, and whose integral curves serve as a natural time coordinate for observers sitting far away from the black hole. This is the typical time coordinate used for the Kerr when expressed in Boyer-Lindquist form.

It is then possible to calculate the total coordinate time elapsed time over the trajectory of any null geodesic γ(r,θ) as,

Δt=ΔIt+a2ΔGt

where,

ΔIt(ro,rs)=It(ro)−It(rs)=∫rsror2Δ(r)+2Mr(r2+a2−aλ)Δ(r)R(r)dr,ΔGt(θo,θs)=Gt(θo)−Gt(θs)=∫θsθocos2⁡θΘ(θ)dθ,Δ(r)=r2−2Mr+a2,R(r)=(r2+a2−aλ)2−Δ(r)[η+(λ−a)2], andΘ(θ)=η+a2cos2⁡θ−λ2cots⁡θ.

One idiosyncracy of this coordinate is that it introduces ambiguities when evaluating the elapsed time of any geodesic terminating at the asymptotic observer. The ambiguity is due to logarithmic and linear divergences in the integral It at ro=∞ which take the form,[1]

limr→∞dtdr≈2Mr+1,

These divergences are indepedent of the nature of the geodesicas.

One way around these ambiguities is for the observer to ask questions that have a regulating effect on the divergences. An example of a valid question is; "what is the relative time delay of the arival of a photon, i, with respect to a reference photon, j";

ti−tj=Δti−Δtj+tsi−tsj,

where tsi and tsj are the emission times of photons i and j respectively.

Another approch is to avoid the divergent quality of It altogether by introducing a regularized version of the integral, I~t, defined as;

ΔI~t(rs,∞)=limro→∞∫rsrodtdrdr−2Mlog⁡(roero2M),

The definition of this integral is possible in practice since It takes the forms of the sums of Elliptic Integrals of the first (F), second (E) and third (Π) kind, as well as logarithms and linear functions of r.

It(r→∞)≃#1F(ϕ(r)∣k)+#2E(ϕ(r)∣k)+#3Π(n;ϕ(r)∣k)+#4log⁡(#5r2)+r+#6

Some of the logarithmic divergence resides in the contribution from the Elliptic Integral of the third kind; a fact which follows from the logarithmic divergence of Π(n;ϕ|k) as n→csc2⁡ϕ. This divergence can be extracted with the connection formula,[2]

Π(α2;ϕ∣k)+Π(ω2;ϕ∣k)=F(ϕ∣k)+cRC((c−1)(c−k),(c−α2)(c−ω2)),

where,

α2ω2=k and c=csc2⁡ϕ

and

Rc(x,y)=1x−ylog⁡|x+x−yy|.

Our convention here differs from [2:1] to be consistent with [3]. The two are related by,

F(ϕ,k)=F(ϕ∣k),E(ϕ,k)=E(ϕ∣k), andΠ(ϕ,n,k)=Π(n;ϕ∣k).

Thus, our regularized time takes the form

ΔI~t(rs,∞)=I~t(∞)−I~t(rs),

with I~t(rs)=It(rs) for finite rs and,

ΔI~t(∞)=#1F(ϕ∣k)+#2E(ϕ∣k)+#3Π~(n;ϕ∣k)+#~4log⁡(#~5)+#6,

where we have used the connection formula to define a regularized Elliptic integral of the third kind by,

Π~(α2;ϕ∣k)=F(ϕ∣k)−Π(ω2,ϕ∣k).
